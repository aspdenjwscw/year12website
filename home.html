How to manage your time?

How to Manage Your Time (Especially for School Stuff)

Time can get away from you fast, especially when school gets busy.
Here are some simple ways to stay on top of everything without stressing too much:

1. Use a Calendar or Planner
Write down when things are due — tests, assignments, projects — all of it.
That way you won’t forget anything and can see what’s coming up.

2. Don’t Do Everything at Once
If something feels huge, break it into smaller steps.
Do a bit each day instead of cramming it all the night before.

3. Make a Routine
Try to study or do homework around the same time every day.
It helps build a habit and makes it easier to focus.

4. Put Distractions Away
Phones, notifications, TikTok — they can wait.
Find a quiet spot and knock your work out quicker.

5. Take Breaks
You’re not a machine. Work for 25–30 minutes, then chill for 5.
Short breaks keep your brain from burning out.

6. Start Sooner, Not Later
Even just starting a task makes it feel less scary.
The earlier you begin, the more time you have to fix things or ask for help.

How to reduce stress

How to Reduce Stress (Especially Around School) How to reduce stress?

Feeling stressed is normal, especially with school and life stuff happening all at once.
But there are easy ways to calm down and feel more in control:

1. Take Deep Breaths
Slow, deep breaths help your body relax.
Try breathing in for 4 seconds, holding for 4, and breathing out for 4.

2. Get Some Fresh Air
Go for a walk, sit outside, or just open a window.
Nature helps clear your mind.

3. Talk to Someone
Don’t keep it all in your head.
Talk to a friend, family member, or teacher. It really helps.

4. Get Enough Sleep
Your brain needs sleep to think clearly and feel calm.
Try to get 8–10 hours if you can.

5. Move Your Body
Even a little bit of exercise like dancing, stretching, or playing a sport
can boost your mood and reduce stress.

6. Do One Thing at a Time
Don’t try to do everything at once.
Focus on one task, finish it, then move on.
It feels way less stressful.

7. Take Short Breaks
If you’re studying or working, take small breaks to rest your brain.
It helps you focus better afterward.

8. Be Kind to Yourself
It’s okay to not be perfect.
Remind yourself that you're doing your best — and that’s enough.
